Restaurant Order Analysis

About this project

In this project, I acted as a Data Analyst for the Taste of the World Cafe; a restaurant that has diverse offerings and serves generous portions. It debuted with a new menu at the start of the year. I have been asked to dig into the customer data to see which menu items are doing well/not well and what the top customers seem to like best. This analysis is for the first quarter of 2023 and was done using MySQL as the main tool for exploring and analyzing the data. The data is obtained from the restaurant_db where we could find 2 tables menu_items and order_details.

Insights

  • The menu contains 32 different dishes that are under 4 categories mainly American, Asian, Mexican, and Italian cuisines.
  • The price range of the dishes is somewhere from $5 to $19.95.
  • There were 5,370 orders placed that contained 12,234 items within the time frame.
  • 23 orders were placed containing more than 12 items, and these were also the top grossing transactions. The greatest total bill amounted to $192.15.
  • The top grossing cuisine is Italian at $49,462.70, while the bottom is American with $28,237.75.
  • The top grossing item is the Korean Beef Bowl with 588 orders and total gross revenue of $10,554.

Recommendations

  • Menu Optimization
    • Highlight popular and high-grossing dishes like the Korean Beef Bowl in marketing campaigns or promotional materials to drive more orders.
    • Analyze the performance of dishes within the American cuisine category to identify opportunities for improvement, such as introducing new dishes or reworking underperforming ones.
  • Upselling and Cross-Selling Opportunities
    • Use the data on high-grossing transactions with over 12 items to develop upselling strategies for smaller orders, such as offering discounts for orders exceeding a certain number of items.
    • Implement cross-category pairings (e.g., Italian and Asian dishes) to encourage larger orders.
  • Pricing Strategy
    • Evaluate the pricing structure of the dishes, especially for the American cuisine category, to ensure competitiveness while maintaining profitability.
    • Consider promotional bundles or combo offers to attract customers to underperforming cuisines.
  • Customer Engagement and Insights
    • Conduct surveys or collect feedback from customers to understand preferences and improve offerings, particularly for the American cuisine category.
    • Offer loyalty rewards or incentives for large orders to encourage repeat business.
